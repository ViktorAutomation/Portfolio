Automatic Post Creation System — Web Page to Telegram & Facebook

An automated pipeline that turns news articles from a website into ready-to-publish posts for Telegram and Facebook — with human approval and content validation steps built in. Humans approve; automations do everything else.

Make.com OpenAI API Telegram Bot Facebook API Google Sheets Human-in-the-Loop

🎯 The Challenge

The client wanted to publish curated news content to Telegram and Facebook consistently, without burning hours each day on monitoring, summarizing, and formatting. But they also weren't comfortable with fully autonomous AI posting — they wanted a human check before anything went live, with all of it tracked in one place.

Goals:
  • Automated monitoring of specific news sources
  • Automatic summarization and post creation from new articles
  • A convenient approve/reject interface for the human reviewer (Telegram)
  • Structured publishing to Telegram and Facebook once approved
  • Central tracking of all posts and statuses in Google Sheets

🧩 The Solution

A pipeline of Make.com scenarios that watches a source, drafts a post with OpenAI, routes it through a Telegram approval bot, validates the approved content, and then publishes to both Telegram and Facebook — with every step logged in Google Sheets.

01
From website to Telegram — drafts & summaries
Scenario 1 monitors a website or RSS feed for new articles. When a new one appears, it:
  • Fetches the article content
  • Summarizes it via the OpenAI API
  • Generates a preliminary post (text, link, optional hashtags)
The draft post is sent to a Telegram bot for the human reviewer to approve or reject — right from their phone.
02
Validation & post preparation
Scenario 2 triggers when the reviewer interacts with the Telegram bot. On approval, the system:
  • Flags the article as selected in Google Sheets
  • Runs additional validation checks (e.g., cross-referencing other sources, Prepl-style content checks)
  • Generates the final post text and stores it in Google Sheets, ready to publish
03
Approval tracking & publishing
Scenario 3 regularly checks Google Sheets for posts marked approved and publishes them automatically to:
  • Telegram (channel or group)
  • Facebook (page)
Publishing timestamps and statuses are logged back to Google Sheets, so the entire history is visible at a glance.
04
Streamlined two-scenario variant Optional
Depending on the client's needs and Make.com operation budget, the three-scenario flow can be consolidated into two:
  • One scenario for monitoring + drafting + approval
  • One scenario for validation + publishing
Same outcome, fewer moving parts, slightly cheaper to run.

📈 Results & Impact

  • End-to-end automation — from article detection to published post, the pipeline handles every repetitive step.
  • Manual workload drastically reduced — no more monitoring sites, copy-pasting articles, or rewriting summaries by hand.
  • Quality stays high — structured approval and validation steps mean nothing publishes without a human sign-off.
  • Full visibility — the Google Sheets log makes it easy to see what was posted, where, when, and with what content.
