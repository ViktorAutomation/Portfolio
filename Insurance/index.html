🎯 The Challenge

This insurer bundles a smart home security system with every home insurance policy — so every customer relationship involves not just a policy, but a physical product to ship, install, register, and activate, plus inspections, billing, claims, and annual renewals. All of it was being run out of Excel.

The breaking point was renewals. Every policy is valid for one year. When it expires, a new policy has to be created and signed. In Excel, the only way to record that was to duplicate the customer — creating a second, third, fourth record for the same person. Past 1,000 policies, the customer list was bloated with duplicates, reporting was unreliable, and tracking who needed what, when, became nearly impossible.

There were no reminders, no notifications, no escalations. Every deadline depended on someone remembering to check a spreadsheet. The work was slow, manual, and error-prone — and it could not scale.

Goals:
  • Eliminate customer duplication on renewal — one customer, many linked annual policies
  • Replace manual spreadsheet tracking with automated reminders and escalations
  • Give every department a clear, real-time view of what needs attention
  • Build something clean and scalable that Excel could never be

🏗 The Architecture

Monday.com is the system of record. A custom Monday app feeds data in; Make.com orchestrates everything behind the scenes; and five external platforms each handle what they do best. The Monday Customer Item ID acts as the immutable identifier across all of them, so when emails, addresses, or policy numbers change, every record stays linked end to end.

✨ The Standout: A Custom Monday App

Most Monday work is board configuration and automation. This went further: a custom Monday app, built with Monday Vibe, that turns customer onboarding from a five-board chore into a single screen.

Before the app, adding a new customer meant manually entering different information into five different boards — Customers, Policies, Applications, Inspections, and Smart Home — and manually creating the links between them. Tedious, slow, and easy to get wrong.

With the app, the manager enters everything once, in one place. The app automatically distributes the records across all five boards and creates every cross-board connection in the background. No board-hopping. No manual linking. One entry, everything wired up correctly.

The custom Monday Vibe app — one screen, every board populated and linked automatically

⚙️ Key Automations

Forty-one Make scenarios drive the system. A few of the most impactful:

Policy renewal lifecycle The headline fix
Customer and policy are modeled as separate, linked records. A customer keeps one record for life; each annual policy is a new linked item. Renewals no longer duplicate the customer — ending the data bloat that made Excel reporting impossible and giving the team a clean, accurate view of every customer's full history.
Inspection ordering — never missed
Home inspections must be ordered on a strict cadence. The system removes any chance of forgetting:
  • Automatically sets each inspection's order date to the next Thursday
  • Thursday morning, emails the manager the full list of inspections due that day
  • Surfaces a dedicated board group so the manager can visually confirm what's due
  • At 3 PM, sends a reminder if any inspection still hasn't been ordered — a safety net before the day ends
Red Flag risk detection Underwriting
When a new customer is added, the system automatically checks customer, policy, billing, and home parameters against acceptable ranges — for example a homeowner over 70, a roof older than 10 years, or coverage limits outside set bands. If anything is out of range, it:
  • Sets a visible Red Flag marking exactly which parameter is out of range
  • Creates a subtask assigned to the right manager to review the flag and policy
  • Automatically removes the Red Flag once that subtask is marked complete
Risk gets caught at intake, routed to a human, and closed out cleanly — no manual monitoring.
Smart-home logistics SLA monitoring
Because every policy ships with a physical security kit, the system watches the fulfillment chain and escalates when a stage stalls — ordered but not shipped within 2 days, shipped but not installed within 5 days, installed but not registered within 2 days, and more. Nothing gets stuck silently.
Department-specific daily reminders
Different departments get different daily digests of exactly what they need to act on — billing discrepancies routed to the accountant, escalations to managers, and so on. Each person sees their own work, not everyone else's noise.
Multi-due-date notifications & escalations
Throughout the policy lifecycle, the system tracks dozens of due dates and surfaces reminders and escalations — via Monday dashboards and Slack — so deadlines are driven by the system, not by memory.
